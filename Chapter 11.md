**Common Pitfalls and Edge Cases**

**Pitfall 1: Pillar Imbalance**

**The mistake:** Over-investing in technology while neglecting talent or process **The result:** Sophisticated tools operated by overwhelmed team with broken processes **The fix:** Advance all pillars together; weakest constrains overall performance

**Pitfall 2: Future-Proofing Paralysis**

**The mistake:** Waiting for "perfect" technology or complete clarity on future state **The result:** Competitors build capabilities while you analyze **The fix:** Build adaptable foundations now; invest 15-20% in future capability exploration

**Pitfall 3: Big-Bang Transformation**

**The mistake:** Attempting simultaneous comprehensive change across all dimensions **The result:** 20-30% success rate; organizational overwhelm **The fix:** Phased implementation achieves 70-80% success through sustainable change

**Pitfall 4: Framework Without Execution**

**The mistake:** Creating comprehensive strategy documents that sit unexecuted **The result:** Investment in planning without capability building **The fix:** 90-day quick wins demonstrate value; governance ensures accountability

**Edge Cases**

**Acquisition during transformation:** Pause to assess combined organization's maturity; opportunity to establish best practices across both entities.

**Market downturn:** Accelerate efficiency initiatives; maintain retention capabilities; moderate expansion investment temporarily.

**Competitive disruption:** If competitors achieve CS leadership first, timeline compression may be necessary despite higher risk.

**Technology platform sunset:** If current vendor is acquired or declining, platform migration becomes urgent; incorporate into transformation roadmap.
